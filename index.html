// Questions

Things people ask us.

What exactly is GEO and why does it matter now? ›
GEO — Generative Engine Optimisation — is the practice of improving how your brand appears inside AI-generated answers. When someone asks ChatGPT "what's the best gym in Nairobi?" or Gemini "which solicitor should I use in London?", the models generate an answer from everything they've learned. GEO makes sure your brand is part of that answer.
Can you guarantee my brand will appear in AI answers? ›
No — and anyone who promises that is misleading you. Generative AI models are probabilistic. What we do is systematically improve every signal that influences model outputs — structured content, entity consistency, citation authority, schema markup — and track the improvement month over month. We promise measurable progress, not a guaranteed position.
How do you measure AI visibility without scraping ChatGPT? ›
We use official developer APIs — OpenAI, Google Gemini, and Perplexity — not the consumer interfaces. These give consistent, reproducible results and are designed for exactly this kind of structured testing. We run a fixed set of buyer prompts in a controlled environment and log every output. It's structured sampling, not scraping.
How long before I see results? ›
SEO improvements typically show in 4–8 weeks. AEO (Google snippets and AI Overviews) in 4–12 weeks. GEO — LLM visibility — takes longer as models update on their own schedules. Expect meaningful signal after 60–90 days. That's why our Growth plan has a 3-month minimum.
Do you work with influencers and creators? ›
Yes — this is a growing vertical. When a brand asks ChatGPT "who are the top sustainable fashion creators in the UK?" or "best fitness influencers in Nairobi?" — does your name appear? We run creator visibility audits and optimise for the exact prompts brands use when scouting talent. Same methodology, aimed at a person instead of a company.
Do you serve businesses in Kenya? ›
Yes. We serve both the UK and Kenya with localised pricing, localised prompt sets, and relevant industry focus. For Kenya: tourism, fintech, FMCG, healthcare and Kenyan creators. The methodology is identical — the prompts and target publications are localised to each market.
